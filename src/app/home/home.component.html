EarthSense

What is EarthSense?

Human health depends on a variety of factors and the air we breathe is not an exception. The question of whether or not global warming is affecting our planet has introduced an even more important concern to the general public; how safe is the air we are breathing? Any equipment that is collecting air quality data is typically sponsored on a federal level from the EPA or on a state level via local weather stations. EarthSense aims to develop a cheap, reliable, and scalable device that collects air quality samples and data, stores this data on a centralized server, and graphs the results across the mapped areas on a website. This enables a more local and consistent analysis of air quality around the deployed area, but paves the way for scalability.

WHY

Why is this important?

There exists a lack of environmental data that prevents the general and local public from making educated decisions about their habits and impacts on the environment. Decisions such as taking a car to campus instead of carpooling or riding the bus, tailgating with the thousands of other Razorback fans for the big game, or taking out the motorcycle for the Bikes, Blues, and Barbecue weekend festival all directly impact the air quality in Fayetteville. Pollutants in the air vary day-to-day based on local events, human activity, and weather patterns.
